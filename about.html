Our Story

Built for Small Business Owners Who Deserve Real Numbers

TrueCostHiring exists because too many employers make their most important financial decision - hiring - based on incomplete information. We built the tool we wished existed.

Why We Built This

It started with a common scenario: a small business owner posts a $55,000 job offer, thinks they are budgeting $55,000, and then gets hit with an unexpected tax bill at year end. The real cost was closer to $70,000. Nobody told them about FICA, FUTA, SUTA, workers compensation, or benefits overhead.

We kept seeing the same story repeated across Reddit threads, small business forums, and accounting communities. Employers were making six-figure hiring decisions based on a single number - the salary - while missing 25% to 40% of the true cost sitting right below the surface.

The mission is simple: Give every small business owner in America a free, accurate, no-jargon tool that shows exactly what a hire will really cost before they sign the offer letter.

What TrueCostHiring Does

TrueCostHiring is a free web-based calculator that computes the true annual cost of hiring a W2 employee versus engaging a 1099 independent contractor. It factors in every mandatory employer cost that does not appear on a job posting but absolutely appears on your tax bill:

  • FICA payroll taxes (Social Security 6.2% + Medicare 1.45%) paid by the employer
  • FUTA federal unemployment insurance (effective 0.6% on first $7,000 of wages)
  • SUTA state unemployment insurance, with real rates for all 50 US states built in
  • Workers compensation insurance, tiered by job risk category
  • Benefits package costs from zero to full coverage

The result is a side-by-side comparison showing what a W2 employee truly costs versus the same person engaged as a 1099 contractor at the same rate - so you can make an informed financial decision, not a guess.

Our Commitment to Accuracy

All tax rates in TrueCostHiring are updated for the 2025 tax year and sourced from IRS publications and state workforce agency rate tables. We use the effective FUTA rate of 0.6% (reflecting the standard SUTA credit), standard new-employer SUTA rates for each of the 50 US states, and NCCI workers compensation classification tiers.

⚠️

Important disclaimer: TrueCostHiring is an educational tool, not professional tax or legal advice. Results are estimates based on standard new-employer rates. Your actual rates may vary based on your claims history, state, and industry. Always consult a licensed CPA or HR professional before making hiring decisions.
